Article the Firstthe apology

My dearest Monalisa,

We were deep in nonsense — mid-joke, laughing at nothing in particular — when you looked at me and made it official:

"Now go write me a letter and apologise, and bring me cake and chocolate — and you come as well."

So here I am. Obeying. Every single word.

Do I know precisely what I'm apologising for? Not a clue. But a queen gave an order, and one does not argue with a masterpiece. So — I'm sorry. Sincerely, formally, gold-trim-and-wax-seal sorry. For whatever it was, and for the far greater crime of not spoiling you sooner.

You asked. So I bring:

the entire Kinder family — Bueno, the little Chocolate bars, Délice, Joy, Surprise, even the Happy Hippo — and the cake you wanted: one absurdly glossy chocolate gâteau glacé, shiny enough to fix your hair in. (For the record: you out-shine the cake. And the cake is very good-looking.)

And your final, most important clause? I'm coming too — soon. Non-negotiable. Non-refundable. Slightly nervous. Arms full of chocolate.

Consider every clause fulfilled, Monalisa — you asked, and I delivered. Because you're ridiculously easy to spoil, and far too much fun to say no to.

Yours, reporting for cake duty —
